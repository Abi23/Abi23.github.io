Projects

DoorDash

DoorDash - Diet Choice

For the final project at Product School, we were asked to imagine ourselves to be the product manager at a company and come up with a feature. I chose Doordash for this project. I introduced a feature called, Diet Choice that allows customers to order healthy food in a quick and efficient manner.


Persona

I would like you to meet Sara. Sara is a busy professional and fitness enthusiast.

Problem

Sara has recently made a decision to follow the paleo diet. At present, There are no filter options for diet plans. You can filter restaurants by cuisine type. She has to select a restaurant and then when she inside the restaurant page, she has to read through the menu description OF SEVERAL ITEMS word by word before placing an order. Sara finds this process of ordering paleo food very time-consuming. This creates friction in the order process.

Solution

To solve this problem, we introduce DIET CHOICE. This feature will help customers like Sara to order food that aligns with their diet plan in a quick and efficient manner. To make this FEATURE possible we need to add diet LABELS to the menu description. So the user does not read menu description word by word.We recommend adding a filter option named ‘diet choice’ that aggregates the restaurants that have health labels. It is better to hand over the responsibility of adding labels to the menu, to the restaurants instead of curating the menu due to liability issues. But this can be discussed further with the legal team.

Market Validation

According to a Door Dash press release in December 2016, healthy eating is the fastest growing customer segment. The data analyzed by Door Dash showed that customers are testing out new diets and eating healthier, with orders from organic restaurants gaining the biggest increase in 2016, followed by orders for paleo food and smoothies. Grub Hub used a combination of dietary tags and a grouped foods that align with specific eating plans and analyzed the order data. There was 370 percent increase in Paleo food orders in 2016 vs. the year prior. GrubHub did not publish a baseline for this study. However, both companies have reported an increase in the order of healthy food items.This clearly shows that it is important to address the pain points of the healthy eating segment.

MVP

For the MVP, I suggest we add the diet label option to the restaurant menu design form used by restaurants. We should also display the menu labels and filter option on the customer-facing website.
